# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and write a bio that reflects your academic interests and goals. |
| 2 | **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my future employer, professor, or parents saw this?" |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings.** Regularly review who can see your posts, your friend/follower list, and your personal information. |
| 4 | **Credit others' work.** When you share articles, images, or ideas, acknowledge the original creator. |
| 5 | **Report abuse and harassment.** Use the platform's reporting tools if you witness or experience bullying, hate speech, or harassment. |
| 6 | **Keep your college email separate.** Use your college email only for academic/professional sign-ups — not for personal social media accounts. |
| 7 | **Cross-check before sharing news.** Verify information from at least two credible sources before forwarding or posting it. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't share personal details publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, Aadhaar number, or financial information on any social platform. |
| 2 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers.** Unknown accounts may be fake profiles used for scams or data collection. |
| 3 | **Don't post or share content that could be hurtful, discriminatory, or offensive.** What seems like a joke can cause real harm and have lasting consequences. |
| 4 | **Don't use social media to vent about professors, classmates, or your institution.** These posts can damage relationships and your academic standing. |
| 5 | **Don't click unverified links** shared via DMs or comments — they are a common method of phishing and account hacking. |
| 6 | **Don't share exam questions, assignment solutions, or confidential academic materials** online. This violates academic integrity policies. |
| 7 | **Don't post your real-time location** or travel plans publicly — this can be a safety risk. |
